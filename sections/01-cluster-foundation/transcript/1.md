# Video: FinOps starts with a knowable cluster; Kind as sandbox

**Time Budget:** 2 min

### Opening

Welcome to the course. Before agents, tagging rules, or cost savings, you need a cluster you can actually see into.

FinOps is governance over live infrastructure — pods, services, storage. Kubernetes does not hand you a bill by team. It hands you objects across namespaces. If you cannot inspect the cluster, you cannot answer: what is running, who owns it, what does it cost?

Section 01 builds that **inspection surface**. No app yet — just a cluster you trust.

---

### Why Kind

We use **Kind** — Kubernetes IN Docker. Real API server, real kubectl — same commands as production. Fast, disposable, no cloud account. Break something? Recreate in thirty seconds.

We will name it `finops-cluster`. One node on purpose; production has hundreds, but the commands are identical.

---

### Close

Three demo beats: create the cluster, carve out team namespaces, confirm the baseline is clean.

FinOps starts with a **knowable** cluster. In the next video we map SkyLine Air teams to namespaces and introduce labels — the keys that allocate cost to the right team.
