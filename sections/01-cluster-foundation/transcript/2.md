# Video: SkyLine Air teams → namespaces; labels as billing keys

**Time Budget:** 2 min

### Opening

Before you run the commands, here is how SkyLine Air organizes work in Kubernetes.

Namespaces are the first boundary — logical boxes for teams and services. In production they drive RBAC, network policies, and quotas. Here they keep five airline teams separated.

---

### The five namespaces

We create five, each mapping to a business slice:

**booking-api** — reservations. **flight-search** — availability. **inventory** — seat and route capacity. **payment** — card processing. **airline** — shared airline resources (you will see why in Section 02).

`kubectl create namespace` is not just making empty folders — you are drawing the org chart onto the cluster.

---

### Labels and the clean baseline

Namespaces give coarse boundaries. **Labels** on Deployments, Services, and PVCs — `owner`, `cost-center`, `environment` — are the billing keys. Finance bills by who owns the workload, not namespace alone. Section 03 enforces this with `tagging-rules.yaml`. For now: **namespaces organize teams; labels allocate cost.**

After setup, the demo confirms the cluster is **empty** — only `kube-system` noise, no pods in `booking-api` or `payment`. That blank canvas matters so Section 02 deploys against a known starting point.

---

### Close
