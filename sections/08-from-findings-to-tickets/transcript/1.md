# Video 1: The Issue Tracker Landscape

**Duration:** 3–4 minutes  
**Slides:** `slides/slide1_tracker_landscape.svg`

---

FinOps findings are useless if nobody acts on them. A console report tells you what is wrong — an issue tracker tells you who fixes it, when, and whether it is done.

In production, teams reach for tools they already use:

**Jira** — the enterprise default. Rich workflows, custom fields, integrations with ITSM and billing systems. Heavy, but familiar in large orgs.

**Linear** — fast, developer-focused. Great for engineering teams that want speed over ceremony. Less FinOps-specific, but excellent for ownership assignment.

**GitHub Issues** — natural when infrastructure lives in Git. Findings become PRs or issues in the same repo as the manifests. Tight loop for GitOps teams.

**Custom lightweight tracker** — what we build in this section. A FastAPI service with a Kanban board, REST for humans, and MCP for agents. No SaaS dependency, full control over the FinOps ticket schema.

Why not just email a report? Because reports disappear. Trackers create accountability: assignee, priority, status, history. FinOps is an Operate-phase discipline — you need work items, not one-off alerts.

Why expose MCP in addition to REST? Agents speak MCP natively. When your FinOps agent finishes a scan, it should call `create_issue` on the tracker MCP server — same payload as a curl to `/create-issue`, but wired for automation.
